Prepare to Kill Your Bed Bugs

Step 1

Close off the room by taping off any gaps or holes that the bed bugs might be able to crawl through or in (e.g., doorways, cracks, windows, and vents).

Step 2

Put all your loose belongings sealed plastic bags. Try to reduce as much clutter as possible, as these items can harbor more bed bugs.

Step 3

Collect your furniture and all other items to the center of the room to make it easier to treat those items and other areas of the room. This will also help contain the infestation.

Step 4

Heat treat your launderable items (e.g., clothes, fabrics, and linens) by loading them into the dryer and cycling them on high heat for a period of time, depending on the items. This will kill all of the bed bugs.

Treat Your Infestation




Step 5

Spray Proof on your beddings, mattress, sheets, and other surrounding areas. Use 1 fl. oz. of spray per 1 to 6 sq. ft. depending on the absorbancy of the material. Leave on undisturbed for 4 hours. You may smell a strong scent while the product works—this is normal.

Step 6

Use Proof to protect your home for the long-term be spraying it on or near cracks, joints, and hidden areas to kill bed bugs through residual contact for up to 2 weeks with a single application.

Step 7

Apply every 1 to 2 weeks for ongoing protection for you and your family.

Prevent Future Infestations

Mattress Encasement
